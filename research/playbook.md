# LinkedIn Organic Content Strategy Playbook (B2B SaaS)

## 1. Overview
This playbook outlines a practical approach to building a LinkedIn organic content strategy for B2B SaaS.

It is based on analysis of 10 practitioners who actively share insights on content, growth, and audience building. The goal is to translate their ideas into a clear, actionable system that can be applied by individuals or small B2B teams.

---

## 2. Step-by-Step Strategy

### Step 1: Define your niche and audience
Start by clearly identifying who you are speaking to and what specific problem you solve.

Strong positioning helps your content resonate with the right audience, while vague positioning leads to low engagement and unclear messaging.

(Source: Katelyn Bourgoin, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/katebour_how-to-build-a-1m-business-as-a-solopreneur-activity-7450516111483293696-YdmF)

(Source: Tas Bober, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tasbober_ok-i-have-to-say-it-customer-journey-mapping-activity-7452687798756720640-3Q38)

---

### Step 2: Create valuable and clear content
Focus on delivering simple, useful ideas that your audience can quickly understand and apply.

Clarity is more important than complexity. Content that is easy to read and immediately valuable performs better than content that tries to sound overly sophisticated.

(Source: Eddie Shleyner, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/eshleyner_15-words-the-simplest-way-to-improve-activity-7452415884410773504-Snit)

(Source: Amanda Natividad, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_your-brands-public-record-is-being-written-activity-7449837643539849216-uPeh)

---

### Step 3: Be consistent rather than perfect
Consistency builds trust over time. Showing up regularly is more important than waiting to publish perfect content.

A sustainable posting rhythm allows long-term growth without burnout.

(Source: Justin Welsh, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justinwelsh_if-you-can-spend-a-decade-showing-up-feeling-activity-7452684724310941696-vog4)

(Source: Lara Acosta, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/laraacostar_you-dont-need-algorithm-hacks-to-grow-on-activity-7449418737713238016-8dep)

---

### Step 4: Prioritize distribution and engagement
Creating content is only part of the strategy. Distribution and interaction are equally important.

Engaging with comments, participating in conversations, and actively sharing content increases visibility and reach.

(Source: Ross Simmonds, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosssimmonds_marketing-activity-7450883303479390208-ruZz)

(Source: Dave Gerhardt, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_how-ramp-is-building-an-ai-pilled-marketing-activity-7449765600937148416-lE-R)

---

### Step 5: Build trust before selling
The goal of content is to build trust, not immediate conversion.

When trust is established, the audience is more likely to engage, follow, and eventually convert.

(Source: Katelyn Bourgoin, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/katebour_top-paid-experts-all-have-one-thing-in-common-activity-7452689224123842560-mq10)

(Source: Chris Walker, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/chriswalker171_success-and-achievements-dont-make-imposter-activity-7449877213589708800-Dq6Q)

## 3. Where Experts Disagree

### Case 1: Posting frequency
- - Justin Welsh: Emphasizes frequent and consistent posting to maintain visibility  
  (Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justinwelsh_if-you-can-spend-a-decade-showing-up-feeling-activity-7452684724310941696-vog4)

- Amanda Natividad: Focus more on quality and brand consistency  
  (Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amandanat_your-brands-public-record-is-being-written-activity-7449837643539849216-uPeh)

**My decision:**  
I lean toward a consistent but sustainable posting schedule (3–4 times per week), as it balances visibility with content quality and reduces burnout risk.

---

### Case 2: Personal vs Educational content
- - Alex Hormozi: Emphasizes delivering clear, practical value through content  
  (Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/alexhormozi_to-everyone-just-starting-out-take-a-picture-activity-7450581283879182337-nLDQ)

- Dave Gerhardt: Mix of personal storytelling and brand voice  
  (Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_4-years-working-on-exit-five-the-worst-feature-activity-7450880170095403009-X3k-)

**My decision:**  
A combination works best. Educational content builds authority, while personal storytelling increases relatability and audience connection.

---

### Case 3: Short vs long-form content
- Eddie Shleyner: Short, concise writing  
  (Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/eshleyner_15-words-the-simplest-way-to-improve-activity-7452415884410773504-Snit)

- Chris Walker: More in-depth explanations  
  (Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/chriswalker171_success-and-achievements-dont-make-imposter-activity-7449877213589708800-Dq6Q)

**My decision:**  
Short-form content is more effective for capturing attention quickly, while long-form content can be used strategically to build deeper understanding and authority.

## 4. What I Rejected and Why

### Idea 1: Chasing viral content
(Source: Justin Welsh, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justinwelsh_great-lives-are-remarkably-simple-if-you-activity-7452322341453320192-ZoAR)

**Why rejected:**  
The post emphasizes simplicity and long-term consistency over chasing quick wins. Focusing on viral content can attract low-quality attention and does not reliably build long-term trust or a relevant audience.

---

### Idea 2: Over-optimizing for AI or automation
(Source: Ross Simmonds, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rosssimmonds_the-answer-economy-g2s-2026-ai-search-insight-activity-7452384243860758529-dlAC)

**Why rejected:**  
While the post highlights the growing importance of AI in content discovery, over-reliance on automation can reduce authenticity, which is critical for building trust on LinkedIn.

---

## 5. My Original Idea

I propose using a “content loop” strategy.

One core idea can be reused across:
- a LinkedIn post  
- a follow-up comment discussion  
- a short-form video  

**Why it could work:**  
This approach increases content lifespan, reinforces key messages, and improves efficiency without requiring constant new ideas.

---

## 6. Weaknesses of This Playbook

- It assumes consistent content creation, which may be difficult for beginners  
- It does not include detailed analytics or performance tracking  
- It is based on observed patterns, not controlled experiments  
- Results may vary depending on industry, audience, and execution quality
