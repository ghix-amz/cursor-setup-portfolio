### Insight
This post introduces a major shift in how value is created in the modern economy, moving from the Information Era to what the author calls the “Frequency Era.”
A key strength of this post is its macro-level perspective. Instead of focusing on tactics or tools, it reframes the role of human capability in a world where AI can automate knowledge and information processing.
The post highlights that qualities such as clarity, creativity, emotional intelligence, and intuition are becoming more valuable, as they are difficult to replicate with AI.
By combining data points with a broader narrative about economic change, the message feels both credible and forward-thinking.
Overall, this post positions the author as a thought leader who is not only responding to current trends, but also anticipating how work and value creation will evolve in the future.

## Post 2

### Insight
This post challenges the assumption that success eliminates self-doubt by showing that imposter syndrome can persist even at high levels of achievement.
A key strength of this post is its ability to normalize a hidden struggle. By highlighting that even successful individuals experience doubt, it reduces the pressure of perfection and makes the message highly relatable.
The post reframes imposter syndrome as a natural part of growth, especially when stepping into new levels of responsibility or visibility.
This reflects a deeper understanding of professional development, where confidence is not the absence of doubt, but the ability to move forward despite it.
Overall, the post positions the author as a leader who understands both the external and internal challenges of success.
