### Insight
This post highlights a major shift from traditional search engines to what the author calls the “answer economy,” where AI systems provide direct answers instead of lists of links.
A key strength of this post is its use of data to support the argument, making the insight feel credible and grounded in real market behavior.
The post reframes the role of marketing from driving clicks to ensuring visibility and credibility within AI-generated answers. This means brands are no longer just competing for attention, but for interpretation.
It also emphasizes the importance of distribution across multiple platforms (reviews, mentions, citations), as these signals influence how AI systems understand and present a brand.
Overall, this post positions the author as forward-thinking, highlighting how AI is fundamentally changing how buyers discover and evaluate products.

## Post 2

### Insight
This post highlights a common mistake in content marketing: focusing too much on creation while neglecting distribution.
A key strength of this post is its clear distinction between “content” and “content marketing,” emphasizing that creating content alone is not enough to drive results.
The post also provides multiple distribution methods, showing that successful content requires consistent promotion across different channels.
This reflects a broader strategic principle: one piece of content can be repurposed and distributed in many formats, increasing its reach and lifespan.
Overall, this post positions the author as a distribution-focused marketer, reinforcing the idea that visibility is just as important as creation in modern marketing.
