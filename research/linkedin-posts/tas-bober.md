### Insight
This post challenges a common assumption in B2B marketing: that results should be immediate and directly measurable through conversions.
A key strength of this post is its use of data to highlight how long and complex the B2B buying journey actually is. It shows that most of the decision-making process happens before buyers ever interact with a sales team.
The post reframes conversion as a lagging indicator rather than the main objective, emphasizing that the real role of marketing is to support buyer research and decision-making over time.
This reflects a more realistic and strategic understanding of B2B marketing, where success comes from building trust and providing value throughout the entire customer journey.
Overall, the post positions the author as a practitioner who understands the gap between how companies measure performance and how buyers actually behave.

### Insight
This post challenges a popular content strategy: using public criticism (teardowns) to demonstrate expertise.
A key strength of this post is its focus on human psychology. It highlights that public criticism often leads to defensiveness rather than trust, which makes it ineffective for building relationships with potential clients.
Instead of relying on negative examples, the post suggests alternative approaches such as highlighting good practices, anonymizing examples, or sharing feedback privately.
This reflects a deeper understanding of how trust is built in B2B marketing, where relationships and perception play a critical role in decision-making.
Overall, the post positions the author as a thoughtful practitioner who prioritizes long-term trust over short-term attention.
